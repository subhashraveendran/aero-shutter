Built for speed and reliability

A ground-up PTP/IP implementation, tuned for the realities of camera Wi-Fi.

Fast, resumable transfer

Streams to disk in 4 MiB chunks over an enlarged TCP window. Interrupted downloads resume where they stopped; memory stays flat on any file size.

Native PTP/IP

Speaks the camera's own ISO 15740 protocol on TCP port 15740 — dual command/event sockets, the full handshake, and transactions. No vendor SDK.

Rock-solid connection

App-level keep-alive, instant disconnect detection, and automatic reconnect with backoff keep the link up through idle periods and Wi-Fi hiccups.

Instant auto-import

Reacts to the camera's live ObjectAdded events — new shots land on your device the moment you take them, plus the camera's "Send to smart device" queue.

Thumbnails in your terminal

Inline JPEG previews via the Kitty / iTerm2 graphics protocols, with an ANSI half-block fallback so previews work in every color terminal.

Smart, deduped library

Imports organize by capture date and are recorded in SQLite, so re-imports skip existing files instantly. Filter by new, RAW, JPEG, or a selection.

Keeps you online

On mobile, the camera socket binds to Wi-Fi while your phone keeps cellular data — no all-or-nothing Wi-Fi takeover. On desktop, any second interface works.

Live over-the-air updates

The mobile app updates itself over the air from GitHub Releases — self-hosted, zero-cost, with automatic rollback safety.

Cross-platform

A single static Go binary for macOS, Linux and Windows, and a Capacitor app for iOS & Android. Try the whole mobile flow in your browser, no camera needed.

Works with your Nikon

Most Wi-Fi Nikon D-series DSLRs and Z mirrorless work out of the box. Popular bodies get tuned profiles; the wider family that Nikon's Wireless Mobile Utility covers connects through a generic PTP/IP profile.

Any camera that speaks PTP/IP over its Wi-Fi — the same protocol WMU uses — connects through the generic profile; the tuned bodies get model-specific thumbnails, chunk sizes and remote-control support.

Connect in three steps

Zero-config — you never type an IP address.

01

Turn on camera Wi-Fi

On the D5300: MENU → Setup → Wi-Fi → Enable. The camera becomes its own access point.

02

Join the network

Tap "Connect to camera" in the app (or join "Nikon_WU2_…" in your phone's Wi-Fi settings).

03

It connects itself

AeroShutter auto-detects the camera and opens a session — no address to enter.
